Why gravity won't be quantized like everything else

A visual, PhD-level tour of the problem, the leading programs, why each stalls, and where the genuinely open frontier is. Calibrated for a particle theorist — graviton = gold wavy line, matter = blue line throughout.

1. The problem isn't "quantize gravity" — it's the UV

Everyone with a QFT background can quantize gravity at the linear level. Split the metric around a background,

$$ g_{\mu\nu} = \bar g_{\mu\nu} + \sqrt{32\pi G}\,h_{\mu\nu}, \qquad [G] = M^{-2}\ \ (\hbar=c=1),$$

and hμν is a perfectly good massless spin-2 field. You get a graviton, a propagator, vertices, the works. The Feynman rules exist. The trouble is what happens in loops.

Fig 1. The graviton lives, but every loop integral is dressed with a factor of G·E². Because G carries inverse-mass-squared dimension, each additional loop costs more powers of energy: the theory is perturbatively non-renormalizable by power counting.

The hard fact: divergences you cannot absorb

In a renormalizable theory (QED, QCD) all UV divergences are mopped up by a finite set of counterterms already present in the Lagrangian. In gravity, dimensional analysis forces new, higher-derivative counterterms at every loop order. The milestones every particle theorist should know:

  • 't Hooft & Veltman (1974): pure gravity is one-loop finite on-shell — the only available counterterms (R², Rμν²) vanish by the vacuum equations of motion. A tantalizing near-miss. Add any matter and even one loop diverges.
  • Goroff & Sagnotti (1986): pure gravity diverges at two loops, with a non-removable counterterm
$$ \Gamma^{(2)}_{\infty} = \frac{1}{\varepsilon}\,\frac{209}{2880}\,\frac{1}{(16\pi^2)^2}\int d^4x\,\sqrt{-g}\; C_{\mu\nu}{}^{\rho\sigma}C_{\rho\sigma}{}^{\alpha\beta}C_{\alpha\beta}{}^{\mu\nu}.$$
Fig 2. The famous C³ (Weyl-cubed) counterterm. Its coefficient is non-zero and physical, so Einstein gravity is genuinely not a fundamental QFT in the Wilsonian sense. You'd need an infinite tower of measured coefficients — zero predictivity.

Three ways to read "non-renormalizable"

(a) Predictivity death. Infinitely many counterterms ⇒ infinitely many measurements to fix them. At E ≳ MPl ≈ 1.2×10¹⁹ GeV they all matter at once.

(b) Unitarity / strong coupling. Tree-level graviton–graviton scattering grows like G·E² = (E/MPl)². The partial-wave unitarity bound is violated near MPl — a signal that new physics (or non-perturbative resummation) must intervene, exactly as WW scattering foretold the Higgs.

(c) Conceptual. Even setting renormalizability aside, gravity is special: the metric is the dynamical background, so there is no fixed spacetime to define S-matrix asymptotics, no preferred time slicing (the "problem of time" in canonical quantization), diffeomorphism-invariant local observables are scarce, and black holes threaten unitarity (information paradox) and bring in horizon thermodynamics with S = A/4G. These are the conceptual reasons a particle theorist's reflexes are not enough.

2. What does work: GR as an effective field theory

Crucially, non-renormalizable ≠ useless. Donoghue's program treats GR as a Wilsonian EFT with the Einstein–Hilbert term as the leading operator and the higher-curvature terms as suppressed by MPl. The non-analytic (long-distance) pieces of loops are unambiguous, parameter-free predictions:

$$ V(r) = -\frac{G m_1 m_2}{r}\left[\,1 + \underbrace{3\frac{G(m_1+m_2)}{r c^2}}_{\text{classical GR}} + \underbrace{\frac{41}{10\pi}\frac{G\hbar}{r^2 c^3}}_{\text{genuine quantum correction}} + \dots\right].$$
The leading quantum correction to Newton's potential is a real, calculable number. So the question is not "is gravity quantum-mechanical?" — perturbative quantum gravity is fine and tested-in-principle below MPl. The open problem is the UV completion: what replaces the EFT once E → MPl.

Everything below is a candidate UV completion (or a claim that the UV looks radically unlike a continuum QFT).

candidate 1 String theory

The string idea attacks divergence (a) and (b) at the root: replace the point interaction vertex — the source of the UV-localized loop integral — with an extended object whose interactions are smeared over the worldsheet. The graviton is not postulated; it is the massless spin-2 mode of the closed string, and general covariance emerges as a worldsheet consistency (Weyl-invariance ⇒ βμν = Rμν + … = 0, i.e. Einstein's equations as the condition for a consistent background).

Animation 2. Left: a point-particle vertex — interaction localized at a single event, the seed of the UV divergence. Right: the closed-string "pair of pants" — the same topology but the splitting is smooth and Lorentz-frame-dependent in where it "happens", which exponentially softens UV behavior. This single picture is the physical heart of why string amplitudes are UV-finite order by order.
What it buys you
  • UV-finite perturbative amplitudes; gravity unified with gauge forces and matter in one framework.
  • A microscopic, parameter-free counting of black-hole entropy S = A/4G for (BPS) extremal holes — Strominger–Vafa, 1996. Genuinely a triumph.
  • AdS/CFT (Maldacena): a fully non-perturbative, manifestly unitary definition of quantum gravity in asymptotically-AdS space via a dual CFT. The most concrete handle we have on the information paradox and holography.
Why people aren't satisfied
  • Requires 10 (or 11) dimensions and, perturbatively, supersymmetry. Compactifying the extra six dimensions yields the landscape: ~10500+ vacua with no known selection principle ⇒ weak predictivity for our low-energy world.
  • It is most rigorously defined with a fixed asymptotic background (Minkowski/AdS). It is not manifestly background-independent, which many regard as the essence of GR.
  • No accepted, fully controlled construction of metastable de Sitter (our accelerating universe). The "swampland" debate is precisely about whether dS even exists in the theory.
  • No distinctive low-energy experimental signature at accessible energies.

candidate 2 Loop quantum gravity & spin foams

The opposite philosophy: keep exactly four dimensions, no extra matter or symmetry, and take background independence as sacred. Recast GR in Ashtekar–Barbero connection variables (a gauge theory of SU(2)), then quantize canonically. The eigenstates are spin networks — graphs labeled by SU(2) reps. The punchline that excites people:

$$ \hat A\,|j\rangle = 8\pi\gamma\,\ell_{\rm Pl}^2\sqrt{j(j+1)}\;|j\rangle,\qquad \hat V\,|{\rm node}\rangle = (\text{discrete spectrum}).$$

Geometry is quantized: area and volume operators have discrete spectra with a built-in minimum length. The path-integral version sums over spin foams (histories of spin networks), e.g. the EPRL amplitude.

Fig 3. Spin networks are an orthonormal basis for the kinematical Hilbert space; edges carry area, nodes carry volume. Spacetime is not a smooth manifold at the bottom — it is a combinatorial, Planck-granular structure.
What it buys you
  • Manifestly background-independent and non-perturbative by construction.
  • A concrete prediction of discrete geometry / minimal length; natural resolution of the big-bang singularity into a "bounce" in the symmetry-reduced setting (loop quantum cosmology).
  • No extra dimensions, no SUSY required.
Why people aren't satisfied
  • The continuum limit is unsolved. Nobody has convincingly shown that the discrete dynamics reproduces smooth 4D GR + a graviton with the right long-distance physics. This is the make-or-break gap.
  • The Hamiltonian constraint (Thiemann) has quantization ambiguities and its physical-state space and dynamics are hard to pin down.
  • Coupling Standard-Model matter is not as natural as in string theory; no unification story.
  • Tension/uncertainty around Lorentz invariance: a literal minimal length risks breaking boost invariance, which is tightly constrained by astrophysics.

candidate 3 Asymptotic safety

Weinberg's idea is the most conservative of all: maybe gravity is a QFT, just not a perturbatively renormalizable one. It could be non-perturbatively renormalizable if the RG flow runs into a non-trivial UV fixed point with a finite-dimensional critical surface. Then only finitely many couplings are free — predictivity is restored despite infinitely many operators.

trajectories pulled into the UV fixed point trajectory leaving the critical surface (un-physical)
Animation 3. RG flow in a Newton-coupling / cosmological-constant plane (schematic). The ★ non-Gaussian fixed point has a finite-dimensional UV-attractive surface (here 2–3 relevant directions). A theory living on that surface stays well-defined up to arbitrarily high energy — that is what "asymptotically safe" means, the analogue of asymptotic freedom but at an interacting fixed point.
What it buys you
  • Stays within ordinary 4D QFT — no new ingredients, no extra dimensions.
  • Functional RG (Wetterich equation) truncations consistently find a fixed point with the right number (~3) of relevant directions across many truncations — suggestive evidence.
  • Potential predictions: a famous (if debated) postdiction-style argument for the Higgs mass; constraints on couplings flowing from the fixed point.
Why people aren't satisfied
  • Truncation reliability. Evidence comes from finite truncations of an infinite-dimensional theory space; demonstrating that the fixed point survives in the full theory (not an artifact) is unresolved.
  • Most computations are Euclidean; continuation to Lorentzian signature and a clean definition of the physical state space is hard.
  • Unitarity. Higher-derivative terms generically bring ghosts; whether the asymptotically-safe theory is unitary (the ghosts are spurious / resummed away) is contested.
  • It addresses the UV-completion question but says little about background independence, singularities, or the information paradox.

candidates 4–6 Discrete & Lorentz-breaking approaches

ProgramCore moveWhy it stalls
Causal Dynamical Triangulations Lattice path integral over causal (foliated) simplicial geometries; sum eiSRegge. Numerically grows a 4D, de Sitter-like universe from nothing — a real success, and shows dimensional reduction to ~2 in the UV. Needs a preferred foliation (causality condition) — tension with full diffeo invariance; reaching a second-order phase transition (continuum limit) and recovering gravitons is still in progress.
Causal sets Spacetime = a discrete partial order (causal relations) + counting ⇒ volume. "Order + number = geometry." Predicted the order of magnitude of Λ via fluctuation arguments before/around its discovery. Recovering local dynamics, a Lagrangian, and matter from pure causal order is very hard; non-locality is generic.
Hořava–Lifshitz Give space and time anisotropic scaling t→bzt, x→bx with z=3 in the UV. Then gravity is power-counting renormalizable as an ordinary QFT. Explicitly breaks Lorentz invariance at high energy; a problematic extra scalar mode, strong-coupling and fine-tuning issues to recover GR + Lorentz invariance in the IR.

Group field theory and tensor models sit alongside these as a "second-quantized" formulation of spin foams / random geometry, sharing both the appeal (background independence) and the central difficulty (continuum limit, phase transition to geometry).

candidate 7 Gravity as emergent / thermodynamic

A different stance: maybe you shouldn't quantize gμν at all, because it is not fundamental — it is a coarse-grained, thermodynamic variable, like temperature or fluid density. Quantizing the metric would then be as misguided as "quantizing" the Navier–Stokes equations.

  • Jacobson (1995): demand δQ = T δS with S = A/4G across every local Rindler horizon, and the Einstein equations fall out as an equation of state. Striking — it says GR's field equations are thermodynamics, not microdynamics.
  • Holographic entanglement (Ryu–Takayanagi); ER=EPR; "entanglement builds geometry": in AdS/CFT, the bulk geometry is reconstructed from boundary entanglement entropy. Spacetime connectivity ↔ quantum entanglement.
  • Verlinde's entropic gravity is the most radical (and most criticized) version.
If gravity is emergent, the right "microscopic" degrees of freedom are unknown — these arguments tell you gravity looks thermodynamic but not what the "atoms of spacetime" are, nor do most of them yet predict new numbers. And whether the metric has its own quantum degrees of freedom is exactly what we'd like to test (see §8).

8. Where the genuinely open / under-exploited frontier is

These are the directions a particle theorist is unusually well-positioned to push, and where I think the marginal returns are highest:

(a) The amplitudes & bootstrap attack — gravity from on-shell consistency

Rather than quantizing a Lagrangian, demand Lorentz invariance + unitarity + locality + analyticity and see what survives. Two threads with real momentum:

  • Double copy / KLT: gravity = (gauge theory)² at the level of amplitudes — graviton numerators are squares of gluon numerators (BCJ). It is computationally transformative and hints that gravity's structure is derived, not fundamental. Under-exploited: a non-perturbative, off-shell, curved-background formulation of the double copy is missing — nailing that could be a genuine reframing of what the metric "is".
  • Positivity / causality bounds & the S-matrix bootstrap: dispersion relations + unitarity put two-sided bounds on the Wilson coefficients of the gravitational EFT (e.g. the sign/size of R³, R⁴ terms). This carves out the "landscape vs swampland" boundary rigorously, from low-energy consistency alone — arguably the most model-independent progress of the last decade and far from exhausted.

(b) Replica wormholes, islands, and the Page curve

Hawking's monotone curve (information lost) Page curve (unitary)
Animation 4. The post-2019 breakthrough: including replica-wormhole saddles and "island" contributions to the gravitational entropy reproduces the unitary Page curve (entropy turns over at the Page time) from a semiclassical gravity computation. We can now extract unitary black-hole evaporation without knowing the full UV theory.

Why it's a frontier, not a closed chapter: the islands prescription works beautifully in low-dimensional / AdS toy models, but the actual information-carrying microstates, and a fully 4D, flat-space, real-astrophysical-black-hole version, remain open. Reconciling "entropy comes from wormhole saddles" with standard quantum-mechanical microstate counting (the "ensemble averaging" puzzle) is live and deep.

(c) Celestial / flat-space holography

AdS/CFT is our one airtight definition of quantum gravity, but our universe isn't AdS. Recasting 4D flat-space scattering as a 2D celestial CFT on the sphere at null infinity — organized by the BMS asymptotic symmetry group and the IR soft theorems (Strominger's "infrared triangle": soft theorems = asymptotic symmetries = memory effect) — is the most promising route to a holographic, background-independent-ish handle on realistic gravity. It is young, technically wide-open, and squarely in amplitudes-theorist territory.

(d) Tabletop tests: is gravity even quantum? (BMV)

The Bose–Marletto–Vedral proposal: put two masses each in spatial superposition; if the gravitational interaction generates entanglement between them, then — under the assumption that local operations + classical communication can't create entanglement — gravity must have non-classical (mediating quantum) degrees of freedom. This is an actual, if heroic, near-future experiment that could falsify "gravity is classical" without ever reaching MPl. Hugely under-appreciated by the high-energy community.

(e) Cross-pollination nobody has finished

  • A Lorentzian, ghost-resolved functional RG for asymptotic safety, cross-checked against the amplitudes/causality bounds — two communities computing the same Wilson coefficients from opposite ends, rarely compared head-to-head.
  • Using CDT/causal-set numerics to test whether the asymptotic-safety fixed point and the observed UV dimensional reduction to ~2 are the same phenomenon — a quantitative bridge between discrete and continuum programs that is mostly conjectural today.
  • Cosmological / GW signatures as a data channel: primordial non-Gaussianity, the GW spectrum, and the inflationary "cosmological collider" as a quantum-gravity-adjacent probe — where a tool like GAMBIT-style global fitting (the codebase this very file sits in!) over BSM+gravity-EFT parameters could turn swampland/positivity bounds into confrontations with data.

The one-paragraph synthesis. Perturbative quantum gravity is a fine EFT and makes real predictions below MPl; the crisis is purely the UV completion plus a cluster of conceptual problems (background independence, time, unitarity of black holes). String theory gives the most complete UV-finite, holographic answer but at the cost of extra dimensions and a non-predictive landscape; LQG/CDT/causal sets keep 4D and background independence but cannot yet grow back smooth GR; asymptotic safety is the most economical but rests on un-controlled truncations. The freshest oxygen is coming not from a new "theory of everything" but from consistency-first methods — amplitudes, double copy, causality/positivity bounds, the island/Page-curve resolution of the information paradox, celestial holography — and, remarkably, from experiment: gravitationally-induced entanglement could tell us within a generation whether the metric must be quantized at all.
